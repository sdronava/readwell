# Overall Application Description:

Goal is to create an application that provides an enhanced, immersive, interactive experience for reading books with assistive multimedia. The source books and documents that we will be targetting initially are text books, technical articles, and technical books that have more than plain text. For example, a typical technical book or article will contain illustrative diagrams, tables, pictures, code, formulas etc.

# Content Model

The application operates as an **open, curated library** of freely available books. There is no commercial or paid content. Publishers, authors, and open-source content creators can upload their freely available works (public domain, Creative Commons, or author-donated titles) to the platform. Once approved, books are available to all registered readers in the shared catalog.

- **Readers** browse and read from the shared catalog.
- **Publishers / Authors** upload and manage their own freely available books.
- **Admins** review and approve uploaded books before they appear in the catalog.
- All content must be freely distributable (public domain, CC licensed, or author-authorized).

# Major Features of Application

1. The app will have the normal features you would expect from an e-reader like Kindle, Nook. For example: Fonts, Single Page or Dual Page, Bookmarks, Notes, Comments, Last read etc.
2. The app will have the ability to read the book (text to speech). It should be possible to control the speed of the audio. The voice should sync with a correspondng scrolling text highlighter. It should be possible to pause the audio only.
3. Either at the discretion of the author or on demand by the user, it should be possible to play a pre-recorded video explaining/summarizing in further detail special sections like a table, picture, piece of code. When pre-recorded, the options should be available in an intuitive way in the app UI. The user reading the app may randomly seek an explanation of a section, illustration etc which should be handled with the help of an appropriate agentic system.

4. The app should be minimal in spite of the features and help user get into a 'flow' of reading complex materials.

5. The app should support modern, popular front ends - browsers, mobile phones and tablets.


# Major Features of Backend

1. The backend is essentially a pipeline to convert popular book and articles that are in PDF or ePUB into a form that the reader app will use. Publishers and authors upload files via a web portal; the backend validates, converts, and registers them into the shared catalog automatically.
